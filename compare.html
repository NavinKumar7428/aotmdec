Quick Visual

Satellite vs Fiber vs Mobile Data

This table highlights the real-world differences between connectivity technologies used in India.

Feature Satellite Internet (LEO) Fiber Broadband Mobile Data (4G/5G)
Latency 20–50 ms 1–10 ms 20–60 ms
Speed 50–300+ Mbps 100 Mbps – 1 Gbps+ Up to 300 Mbps
Coverage Near-global Urban & semi-urban Mostly urban
Deployment Time Very fast Slow (cable laying) Moderate
Reliability High (weather dependent) Very high Medium (congestion)
Best For Remote & backup connectivity High-density areas Mobile users

Quick Visual Comparison

Strengths and limitations at a glance

Satellite Internet

Best for remote areas, disaster recovery, maritime, and aviation connectivity.

Fiber Broadband

Delivers highest speeds and stability in cities and industrial zones.

Mobile Data

Ideal for personal mobility, quick access, and temporary connectivity.

Which One Should You Choose?

There is no single perfect solution. Modern networks often combine satellite, fiber, and mobile technologies to deliver the best experience.

  • Satellite for coverage & resilience
  • Fiber for speed & capacity
  • Mobile for flexibility
