How It Works

A simplified flow:

  1. Data travels from user terminal to satellite (uplink).
  2. Satellite relays to a gateway/ground station or inter-satellite link.
  3. Gateway connects to the internet backbone and sends data back (downlink).

LEO constellations use many small satellites and inter-satellite links to reduce latency and increase capacity.




Common Use Cases

Satellite internet enables connectivity in diverse environments:

Remote Villages & Islands

Provides broadband to areas where fiber is not feasible.

Maritime & Shipping

Supports ships and offshore platforms with reliable internet.

Emergency Response

Rapidly deployable networks after natural disasters.

Education & Telemedicine

Supports remote learning and healthcare services.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common queries about satellite internet.

GEO satellites stay fixed relative to Earth and cover larger areas but with higher latency. LEO satellites orbit quickly, need constellations, but offer lower latency.

LEO constellations and inter-satellite links reduce the physical distance for data travel, minimizing latency.
