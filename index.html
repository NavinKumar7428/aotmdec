Satellite Internet in India

Reliable connectivity beyond terrestrial infrastructure — learn what it is, why it matters for India, and how it compares with fiber & mobile data.

How it works

What is Satellite Internet?

Satellite internet delivers broadband connectivity by linking a user terminal to satellites in orbit — relaying data between the ground station, space segment, and user equipment.

  • Two main bands: GEO (geostationary) and LEO (low-earth-orbit)
  • User terminals / dishes connect to spacecraft for uplink/downlink
  • Can serve remote areas where fiber is not viable
Quick facts

Latency: GEO higher (~600ms), LEO lower (~20–50ms)

Coverage: Near-global for major constellations

Typical speeds: 50 Mbps – 300+ Mbps (varies)

How It Works

A simplified flow:

  1. Data travels from user terminal to satellite (uplink).
  2. Satellite relays to a gateway/ground station or inter-satellite link.
  3. Gateway connects to the internet backbone and sends data back (downlink).

LEO constellations use many small satellites and inter-satellite links to reduce latency and increase capacity.

Why Satellite Internet is Important for India

India's geography and digital divide make satellite internet strategically important:

Remote Connectivity

Hilly, island and tribal regions often lack fiber infrastructure.

Disaster Resilience

Quick-deploy satellite links after natural disasters restore comms.

Inclusive Access

Brings education, healthcare & commerce to underserved communities.

Benefits

Rapid Deployment

No need to lay cable — terminals can be installed quickly.

Wide Coverage

Covers remote and maritime areas where other networks fail.

Redundancy

Can act as backup to fiber & cellular networks for mission-critical services.

What is Satellite Internet — Details

Satellite internet uses radio links between earth stations and satellites. GEO sats remain fixed relative to earth, LEO satellites orbit quickly and require constellations for continuous coverage. Modern LEO systems lower latency and increase throughput with many satellites and ground gateways.
