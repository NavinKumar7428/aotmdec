What is Satellite Internet?

Satellite internet is a type of wireless broadband that delivers internet access using satellites orbiting the Earth instead of cables or mobile towers. Data is transmitted between the user, satellite, and ground stations.

  • Two main bands: GEO (geostationary) and LEO (low-earth-orbit)
  • User terminals / dishes connect to spacecraft for uplink/downlink
  • Can serve remote areas where fiber is not viable
Quick Facts

Latency: GEO higher (~600ms), LEO lower (~20–50ms)

Coverage: Near-global for major constellations

Typical speeds: 50 Mbps – 300+ Mbps (varies)

What is Satellite Internet — Details

Satellite internet uses radio links between earth stations and satellites. GEO sats remain fixed relative to earth, LEO satellites orbit quickly and require constellations for continuous coverage. Modern LEO systems lower latency and increase throughput with many satellites and ground gateways.
